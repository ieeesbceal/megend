About MEGEND 2025

MEGEND is an event organized by the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society Student Branch Chapter at the College of Engineering Attingal.

This hybrid-mode event is designed to promote hands-on learning, innovation, and membership development in the IEEE RAS community through interactive workshops, expert talks, and strategic networking..

Join us for an immersive experience featuring keynote addresses by distinguished guests, sessions with industry experts, and hands-on workshops designed to enhance your technical skills.

Workshops

Explore future-ready skills through immersive, hands-on technical workshops.

Getting Started with ROS

Introduction to Robot Operating System (ROS) for robotics applications including simulation, control, and deployment.

Industrial Automation

Hands-on with PLC and SCADA systems for automation in industries, covering sensors, actuators, and control logic.

AI Sign Language Interpretation

Develop AI-powered systems for interpreting sign language in real-time using computer vision and neural networks.

Maker's Bootcamp

Build your first 3D printer from scratch, learning mechanical assembly, calibration, and basic printing techniques.

Circuit Crafting

Learn DIY PCB design and etching techniques using basic tools and safety practices for rapid prototyping.

Team Communication

Icebreaking and communication workshop tailored for technical teams to enhance collaboration and leadership.

Special Features

Discover engaging experiences beyond workshops and talks

Ice Breaking Session

Kickstart your networking journey with team-building activities and games led by Jithin K Jose to foster collaboration and fun.

Treasure Hunt

A thrilling campus-wide hunt combining clues, tech trivia, and teamwork. Compete, collaborate, and win exciting goodies!

Cultural Night

Unwind after a day of learning with performances, music, and vibrant cultural showcases under the stars.

Who Should Attend

MEGEND 2025 welcomes curious minds from diverse backgrounds united by a passion for technology, innovation, and learning.

Engineering Students

Students pursuing B.Tech or M.Tech in domains like robotics, electronics, computer science, and more.

Innovators & Makers

DIY enthusiasts, tinkerers, and project builders passionate about hardware, automation, and creative problem-solving.

Researchers

Individuals involved in research, academic studies, or emerging technologies across disciplines.

Student Leaders & IEEE Members

Tech club members, IEEE student volunteers, and those driving change in campus communities.
